# 6. Design Scope

**Part:** 1 — Philosophy  
**Status:** Draft  
**Type:** Informative / Scope

IdeaMark intentionally defines reusable structural representations.

It intentionally does not define:

- meaning
- universal ontologies
- domain vocabularies
- cognitive models
- fixed interpretations
- future limits of intellectual activity

These evolve outside IdeaMark.

## Openness to Future Intellectual Activity

IdeaMark intentionally avoids defining the limits of intellectual activity.

Instead, it defines reusable structures that allow future intellectual activities—performed by humans, AI, or their collaboration—to emerge from authoritative original sources under new situations and projections.

## Design Rationale

If IdeaMark attempted to define meaning, cognition, or universal vocabularies, it would become tied to the interpretive assumptions of a particular time, domain, or community.

By defining only reusable structural access to original sources, IdeaMark remains open to future AI systems, media forms, projections, and intellectual practices.
